Community Stories: Share Your Shitbox Shuffle Moment

Every session has the potential for something memorable. An unexpected rematch. A trash-talker who turned into a friend. A match that went so sideways it became a story worth telling. This is where those go.

What we publish

We're looking for genuine, human stories from Shitbox Shuffle sessions. They don't need to be dramatic. They need to be real. Categories we're specifically interested in:

  • Unexpected connections: Someone you matched with randomly who you ended up talking to for an hour. A match that started as a card game and turned into an actual conversation.
  • Wild game moments: The blackjack hand you shouldn't have played but did. The poker bluff that somehow worked. The checkers comeback from nothing.
  • Funny or absurd: Matches that went off-script in ways that are genuinely funny rather than inappropriate.
  • The "I was wrong about this" story: Coming in skeptical of the platform or a game, and changing your mind mid-session.
  • Long-running rivals: Getting matched with the same person repeatedly. Rivalries that developed over multiple sessions.

What we don't publish

  • Stories that identify another user by name or unique details without their consent
  • Content that involves describing inappropriate behavior from other users—report those through Support instead
  • Complaints framed as stories (we have a support channel for issues)
  • Stories that reference circumventing age verification or geographic restrictions
  • Anything a reasonable person would consider harassment of another player

How to submit

Submissions go to our support channel with the subject line "Community Story Submission." Email via the support page and include:

  1. Your story — 150 to 600 words. Write it how you'd tell a friend. Don't over-edit.
  2. Your display name or a pseudonym — we publish with a first name or handle only unless you want full attribution
  3. The game involved (if applicable)
  4. Whether you want to be contacted before we publish

We read everything submitted. Response time varies—we'll reach out if your story is selected for publication.

Story prompts to get you started

If you're not sure where to start, try answering one of these:

  • What's the most surprising thing that happened in a match?
  • Describe a match where you thought you'd won, then didn't, or vice versa.
  • What did you learn about a stranger in the span of one game?
  • Describe the best or worst bluff you've ever attempted on Shitbox Shuffle.
  • If someone asked "what is Shitbox Shuffle" and you had one story to explain it, what would that story be?
  • What's the funniest misunderstanding that happened in a match?

What happens after you submit

We read all submissions. If your story is selected:

  • We'll reach out to confirm you still want it published
  • We may do light editing for clarity and length—we'll show you any significant edits before publishing
  • It'll appear in the Community Spotlight section of the blog
  • We'll attribute it by your display name, a pseudonym, or "Anonymous" per your preference

We don't pay for story submissions. This is a community feature, not a paid content program.

A note on privacy

When you submit a story:

  • Don't include personal information about other players that they haven't consented to share
  • We treat your submission as private until it's published or we confirm we won't use it
  • We won't publish your contact email address
  • If your story is not selected, we don't archive it beyond the review period

Review our Privacy Policy for how we handle submitted information generally.

Game played: [Game name] | Session: [approximate timeframe]

[Story in the player's words, lightly edited]
