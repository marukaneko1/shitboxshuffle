Player Safety

Responsible Gaming

Shitbox Shuffle is entertainment. We want you to play within your means, know your limits, and leave the table when the game stops being fun.

⚠ Need Help Now?

If you or someone you know is struggling with problem gambling, call the National Problem Gambling Helpline: 1-800-522-4700 (24/7, free, confidential). Text "HELLO" to 522-4700 or chat at ncpgambling.org/chat.

Play Within Your Limits

Gambling should never be a way to make money or escape problems. Before you play, decide how much you can afford to lose and treat that as the cost of entertainment — not an investment.

  • Set a strict budget before each session and stop when it's gone
  • Never chase losses — a losing streak doesn't guarantee a win
  • Don't play under the influence of alcohol or when emotionally distressed
  • Take regular breaks — sustained play impairs decision-making
  • Keep gambling separate from money for bills, food, and rent

Signs of Problem Gambling

Problem gambling can develop gradually. Watch for these warning signs in yourself or others:

  • Spending more time or money gambling than intended
  • Feeling restless or irritable when trying to cut back
  • Gambling to escape stress, anxiety, or depression
  • Lying to friends or family about gambling activity
  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to gamble
  • Failed attempts to stop or control gambling

Tools We Provide

Your account dashboard gives you direct access to these controls at any time:

Deposit Limits

Set daily, weekly, or monthly deposit caps. Limits take effect immediately and can only be decreased in real-time — increases require a 72-hour cooling-off period.

Set Limits

Session Reminders

Enable pop-up reminders every 30, 60, or 90 minutes of continuous play. A brief break can help you reassess your session with a clear head.

Configure

Take a Break

Lock your account for 24 hours, 7 days, or 30 days. During a break, you cannot deposit, wager, or enter matches. Your balance is preserved.

Request Break

Self-Exclusion

Permanently close your account and block access for a minimum of 12 months. This cannot be reversed during the exclusion period. Contact support to request self-exclusion.
